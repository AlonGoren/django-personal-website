About me

My research focuses on epigenomic mechanisms, and merges basic biology, technological innovations and computational analyses. My major interest is in developmental-epigenomic questions – explorations of chromatin regulatory networks during mouse early embryogenesis.

I am building a group at the Broad Technology Labs (BTL) — the department of The Broad Institute of Harvard and MIT for promoting biotechnological innovations. Driven by the complex nature of developmental-epigenomic questions — e.g., the minimal number of cells in each embryo, dynamic cellular fate-decisions, or the organization of chromatin regulators (CRs) — my group will establish and apply high sensitivity and high-throughput techniques for systematic studies of genome-wide chromatin organization. My goal is to leverage these innovations to enable my group as well as the broader scientific community to study chromatin in uncharted settings.

I firmly believe that communicating and disseminating of knowledge is a major component of science, and I am involved in mentoring students, presentations of webinars (e.g. Chromatin/ChIP-seq), outreach programs and workshops (e.g. 2014-BroadE).

I am looking for motivated postdoctoral applicants to join my group, and for a joint postdoc position (Israel-Broad Institute) between my and Simon Lab, as detailed here.
