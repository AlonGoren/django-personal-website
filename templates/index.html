About me

I am a group leader at the Broad Technology Labs (BTL) — the department of The Broad Institute of Harvard and MIT for promoting biotechnological innovations. My research focuses on epigenomic mechanisms, and merges basic biology, technological innovations and computational analyses. My major interest is in developmental-epigenomic questions – explorations of chromatin regulatory networks during mouse early embryogenesis.

Driven by the complex nature of developmental-epigenomic questions — e.g., the minimal number of cells in each embryo, dynamic cellular fate-decisions, or the organization of chromatin regulators (CRs) — my group is establishing and applying high sensitivity and high-throughput techniques for systematic studies of genome-wide chromatin organization. Our goal is to leverage these innovations to enable my group as well as the broader scientific community to study chromatin in uncharted settings.

I firmly believe that communicating and disseminating of knowledge is a major component of science, and I am involved in mentoring students, presentations of webinars (e.g. Chromatin/ChIP-seq), outreach programs and workshops (e.g. 2014-BroadE).

I am looking for motivated postdoctoral applicants for joint positions with Itamar Simon's group and with Raul Mostoslavsky's group.
